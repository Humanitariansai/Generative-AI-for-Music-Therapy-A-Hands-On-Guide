# **Course Structure & Pedagogy**

Each Humanitarians AI YouTube course follows a **"Learn ‚Üí Build ‚Üí Teach"** cycle:

1. **Start with an Outline**
   Volunteers begin with a structured outline (like the 30-chapter book outline provided here) and refine it collaboratively.

2. **Develop Hands-On Labs First**
   Before any lecture videos are recorded, the course repository includes:

   * Jupyter notebooks
   * Code labs for Suno, MusicGen, MIR, sensor integration
   * Audio datasets
   * Signal-processing demos
   * Applied therapeutic music generation workflows

3. **Use the Humanitarians Courses GPT**
   Volunteers generate:

   * Lecture scripts
   * Slides
   * Lab guides
   * Code tutorials
   * Explanations, metaphors, analogies, demos

4. **Produce YouTube Lectures**
   Once labs & notebooks are complete, volunteers record and upload:

   * Concept walkthroughs
   * Code-along music generation demos
   * Sensor-integration labs
   * Interviews with therapists and researchers

5. **Continuous Updating**
   New fellows and contributors improve the course, adding:

   * New Suno workflows
   * New therapeutic metadata examples
   * Real-time demos using wearables
   * Python feature-extraction tools

# **Part I ‚Äî Foundations of the Course Topic**

---

## **Module 1 ‚Äî Introduction to Generative AI for Music Therapy**

Covers:

* Why generative AI matters for therapeutic contexts
* How metadata shapes therapeutic impact
* Music therapy foundations
* Clinical, emotional, and neurophysiological motivations

---

## **Module 2 ‚Äî Music Therapy Principles & Neuroscience Basics**

Includes:

* Rhythm, entrainment, breathing synchronicity
* Memory, emotion, limbic responses
* Therapeutic parameters (tempo, timbre, harmony, affect)

---

## **Module 3 ‚Äî Musical Metadata & Computational Musicology**

Topics:

* Metadata fundamentals (BPM, mode, valence/arousal)
* Psychoacoustics
* Core MIR workflows used in clinical research

---

# **Part II ‚Äî Technical Skills, Methods & Practice**

---

## **Module 4 ‚Äî Generative Audio Models (Suno, MusicGen, Diffusion, DDSP)**

Includes:

* Prompting methods
* Architectural intuition
* Embedding-based conditioning

---

## **Module 5 ‚Äî Therapeutic Music Generation Workflows**

Hands-on creation of:

* Anxiety-reduction tracks
* Sleep soundscapes
* Motor rehab / gait entrainment audio
* Pediatric / neurodivergent-friendly tracks

---

## **Module 6 ‚Äî Metadata Extraction, Recommendation & Troubleshooting**

Students learn:

* BPM/key extraction
* Timbre and spectral modeling
* Valence/arousal estimation
* Creating therapeutic recommender systems
* Debugging audio generation issues

---

# **Part III ‚Äî Advanced Topics, Adaptive Systems & Impact**

---

## **Module 7 ‚Äî Physiology, Wearables & Closed-Loop Systems**

Topics:

* HRV, EEG, GSR, respiration
* Real-time adaptation pipelines
* Multimodal fusion

---

## **Module 8 ‚Äî Social Impact Lab: Music Therapy Across Lifespans**

Students explore:

* Neurological care (stroke, Parkinson‚Äôs)
* Mental health (PTSD, depression, anxiety)
* Pediatric, NICU, ASD, ADHD applications
* Chronic pain and medical environments

## **Module 9 ‚Äî Capstone: Build an Adaptive Therapeutic Music Tool**

Each learner builds:

* A personalized playlist generator

Capstones must explicitly align with **AI for Good** principles.
